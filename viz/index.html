STEP 01

Planck Spectrum

Spectral radiance of a blackbody emitter. Drag temperature to shift the peak, set bandgap to see how much radiation the cell can actually use.

B(λ,T) = (2hc²/λ⁵) · 1/(e^(hc/λkT) − 1)
STEP 02

Irradiance Field I(x,y)

Spatial power distribution on the cell surface. The center gets the most — edges fall off by inverse-fourth power. The scalar field that drives all the MVC.

I(x,y) = (σT⁴·A_e·d²) / (π·(x²+y²+d²)²)
STEP 03

Gradient Field ∇I

Direction and rate of steepest irradiance change at every point. Arrows point toward the hot spot. Magnitude peaks at ρ = d/√3.

∇I = −4C·(x,y) / (x²+y²+d²)³
